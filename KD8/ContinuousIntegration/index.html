Guiding Points

Continuous Integration is not..

  • A scheduler
  • Meant to manage environments
  • A system administration dashboard
  • Interactive

Continuous Integration is..

  • A methodology
  • Part of the development process
  • Intended to improve software during all phases of the process cycle

Before starting, there are some points about Continuous Integration that are relevant to this presentation.

Continuous Integration is not a tool or a scheduling system. You could probably accomplish similar with a scheduling system, but the advantages of Continuous Integration are not in scheduling. It is a development methodology for iterative and incremental development processes; therefore, the advantages of Continuous Integration come out of your development process. When you shape your development process to include a system that ritualistically builds, tests, and packages your software product, you immediately see the advantages. It isn't as simple as just periodically building your software and testing it with automation.
